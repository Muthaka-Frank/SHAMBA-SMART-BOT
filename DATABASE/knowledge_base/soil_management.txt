SOIL MANAGEMENT AND FERTILIZER GUIDE FOR KENYAN FARMERS
=========================================================
Kenya Agricultural and Livestock Research Organization (KALRO)
Highlands and Semi-Arid Regions

---

UNDERSTANDING YOUR SOIL
========================

Soil pH plays a critical role in nutrient availability. Test your soil before planting.

pH Scale for Farming:
- Below 5.5 : Very acidic — most nutrients are locked. Apply lime.
- 5.5 – 6.0 : Slightly acidic — suitable for tea, coffee, Irish potatoes.
- 6.0 – 6.8 : Ideal range — suitable for maize, beans, vegetables.
- 6.8 – 7.0 : Neutral — suitable for most crops.
- Above 7.5 : Too alkaline — apply sulphur to lower pH.

SOIL INDICATORS FARMERS CAN OBSERVE:
- Dark soil with good crumb structure = high organic matter (good)
- Reddish, compacted soil = low organic matter, likely acidic
- Pale yellow soil with poor structure = leached, likely needs lime + compost
- Waterlogged, sticky clay soil = poor drainage, needs raised beds

---

LIMING (CORRECTING ACIDIC SOILS)
==================================

When to apply: If crops show yellowing (chlorosis) despite fertilizer, or maize tassels 
die young, the soil may be too acidic.

Agricultural Lime (Calcium Carbonate, CaCO3):
- Rate: 2 tonnes/acre for pH below 5.5
- Apply 2–3 months before planting so lime has time to react
- Broadcast evenly and incorporate by ploughing

Dolomite Lime (adds both Calcium and Magnesium):
- Use if soil is deficient in both Ca and Mg
- Rate: 1.5–2 tonnes/acre

DO NOT apply lime and fertilizer at the same time — wait at least 2 weeks between applications.

BASAL FERTILIZERS (APPLIED AT PLANTING)
========================================

DAP (Di-Ammonium Phosphate — 18-46-0):
- Promotes root development and early plant establishment.
- Apply in the planting furrow or hole at planting: 50kg per acre
- Mix lightly with soil before placing the seed on top to avoid burning.
- Works best in soils with pH 6.0–6.5.

CAN (Calcium Ammonium Nitrate — 26N):
- Fast-release nitrogen source. Use as top dressing.
- Do NOT mix with DAP in the hole — apply separately.
- Timing: 4–6 weeks after germination (when plant is knee-high for maize)
- Rate: 50kg per acre. Apply in a ring around the base or in a furrow beside the row.

NPK 17:17:17 (All-purpose fertilizer):
- Use when a balanced supply of N, P, and K is needed.
- Suitable for vegetable gardens and tomatoes.
- Rate: 50–75kg per acre at planting.

Urea (46-0-0):
- High nitrogen, for leafy growth.
- Use as second top-dressing 6–8 weeks after CAN.
- Rate: 25–30kg per acre. Apply when soil is moist and incorporate.
- DANGER: Do not apply urea in dry conditions — it volatilizes (evaporates) and is wasted.

ORGANIC FERTILIZERS AND SOIL AMENDMENTS
========================================

Farmyard Manure (FYM):
- Best type: Well-composted cattle or chicken manure.
- Fresh manure can burn roots — compost for at least 3 months.
- Rate: 5–10 tonnes per acre, incorporated at planting.
- Benefits: Improves water retention, adds micronutrients, feeds soil organisms.

Compost:
- Make from crop residues, kitchen waste, and manure.
- Layer: green (nitrogen-rich) + brown (carbon-rich) materials.
- Wet and turn every 2–3 weeks. Ready in 2–3 months.
- Apply at 3–5 tonnes per acre.

Green Manure:
- Plant legumes (tithonia, dolichos, crotalaria) between crop seasons.
- Chop and incorporate into the soil when flowering.
- Fixes atmospheric nitrogen, improves soil structure.

Vermicompost (Worm Castings):
- Very concentrated. Apply at 0.5 tonne per acre.
- Excellent for vegetable nurseries.

MICRONUTRIENT DEFICIENCIES
============================

Zinc Deficiency:
- Symptoms: White/pale striping on young maize leaves, stunted growth.
- Treatment: Foliar spray with Zinc Sulphate (ZnSO4) — 2g/litre.

Iron Deficiency:
- Symptoms: Yellowing of young leaves while veins remain green (interveinal chlorosis).
- Treatment: Foliar spray with FeSO4 (iron sulphate) — 1g/litre.

Boron Deficiency:
- Symptoms: Distorted, thickened leaves; poor fruit set in tomatoes and beans.
- Treatment: Dissolve 0.5g Borax in 1 litre warm water, dilute further and spray.

Magnesium Deficiency:
- Symptoms: Yellowing between leaf veins on older leaves (lower leaves first).
- Treatment: Spray with Epsom Salt (MgSO4) — 5g/litre.

WATER MANAGEMENT
================

Mulching:
- Apply 10–15cm of dry grass, maize stalks, or straw around plants.
- Conserves soil moisture during dry spells.
- Suppresses weeds.
- Prevents soil splash that spreads fungal diseases.

Irrigation Timing:
- Water early morning to reduce evaporation and fungal disease risk.
- Avoid afternoon irrigation in humid areas.
- For tomatoes: drip irrigation is ideal to keep foliage dry.

Rainwater Harvesting:
- Dig half-moon catchments (zai pits) on sloped land.
- Plant trees on contour to slow water runoff.

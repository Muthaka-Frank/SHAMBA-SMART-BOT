PEST CONTROL GUIDE FOR KENYAN FARMERS
=======================================
Integrated Pest Management (IPM) — KALRO / FAO Approach
Focus: Use cultural, biological, and chemical controls in that priority order.

---

PHILOSOPHY OF INTEGRATED PEST MANAGEMENT (IPM)
===============================================
1. PREVENTION first: healthy soil, resistant varieties, proper spacing.
2. MONITORING: Scout fields at least twice per week. 
   A pest seen early is much cheaper to treat than an outbreak.
3. BIOLOGICAL CONTROL: Use natural enemies where possible.
4. CHEMICAL CONTROL: Only as a last resort, use targeted pesticides.

Economic threshold: Only spray when pest levels are high enough to cause economic damage.
   - Maize: 1 fall armyworm per 2 plants = action threshold
   - Beans: 2+ aphid colonies per plant = action threshold

---

MAJOR INSECT PESTS
==================

1. FALL ARMYWORM (Vamizi / Kamande — Spodoptera frugiperda)
   Crops affected: Maize, sorghum, millet
   Signs: Frass (dark excrement) in leaf whorls, ragged holes, stripped cobs.
   Economic damage: Can destroy 73% of yield if untreated.
   
   BIOLOGICAL CONTROL:
   - Braconid and Ichneumon wasps parasitize eggs and larvae (protect them).
   - Bt (Bacillus thuringiensis var. kurstaki) spray — safe for humans and bees.
   - Green lacewings and ground beetles eat young larvae.
   
   CULTURAL CONTROL:
   - Plant early (first rains) to avoid peak moth season.
   - Apply clean sand + wood ash into plant funnel at early instar stage.
   - Pheromone traps: 5 traps per hectare to monitor and mass-trap adult males.
   - Push-pull companion planting: plant Desmodium under maize + Napier grass border.
   
   CHEMICAL CONTROL (last resort):
   - Emamectin Benzoate 1.9EC: 0.4ml/litre — effective on early instars.
   - Chlorpyrifos 480EC: 2ml/litre — use if outbreak is severe.
   - Dimethoate 40EC: 2ml/litre — systemic action.
   Apply in the morning or evening. Wear gloves and mask.

2. APHIDS (Vidung'ata / Tunutu)
   Crops affected: Beans, tomatoes, maize, vegetables
   Signs: Clusters of small soft-bodied insects on growing tips/undersides; 
   sticky honeydew; sooty mould develops; plants curl and wilt.
   
   BIOLOGICAL: Ladybirds (ladybugs) and lacewings eat aphids — do not kill them!
   CULTURAL: Strong water jet to knock aphids off; remove heavily infested shoots.
   CHEMICAL: Imidacloprid 200SL: 0.5ml/litre; or Dimethoate 40EC: 2ml/litre.
              Pyrethrin (organic): spray in evening to protect pollinators.
   
   Ant management: Ants protect aphids from natural enemies. Apply sticky barrier band 
   (e.g., Tanglefoot) around plant stems to stop ants from climbing.

3. WHITEFLY (Inzi nyeupe — Bemisia tabaci)
   Crops affected: Tomatoes, cassava, sweet potato, beans
   Signs: Tiny white insects under leaves. Leaves yellow and curl. Black sooty mould.
   Danger: Vector of TYLCV (Tomato Yellow Leaf Curl Virus) and cassava mosaic.
   
   CULTURAL: Yellow sticky traps (25 per hectare). Reflective silver mulch repels adults.
   CHEMICAL: Spiromesifen 240SC: 0.8ml/litre; Buprofezin 25WP: 1.5g/litre (IGR mode).
              Rotate products every 2 sprays to prevent resistance.

4. RED SPIDER MITE (Utitiri — Tetranychus urticae)
   Crops affected: Beans, tomatoes, cassava, maize (especially in dry season)
   Signs: Fine webbing on leaf underside; leaves have yellowish, bronze speckle pattern; 
   leaves drop. Mites are tiny (0.5mm) red or greenish dots.
   
   CULTURAL: Increase field humidity. Water stressed plants are more susceptible.
   BIOLOGICAL: Phytoseiid predatory mites — encourage by avoiding broad-spectrum pesticides.
   CHEMICAL: Abamectin 1.8EC: 0.5ml/litre; Spiromesifen 240SC: 0.8ml/litre.
             NOTE: Many standard insecticides do NOT control mites — use miticide-specific products.

5. THRIPS (Vidung'ata vya pembe)
   Crops affected: Onions, beans, maize, flowers
   Signs: Silvery/bronze streaks on leaves; scar tissue on onion leaves; 
   deformed flowers; vector of TSWV (Tomato Spotted Wilt Virus).
   
   CULTURAL: Blue sticky traps (thrips are attracted to blue). Remove weeds.
   BIOLOGICAL: Predatory mites (Amblyseius cucumeris) eat thrips larvae.
   CHEMICAL: Fipronil 200SC: 0.5ml/litre; Spinosad 480SC: 0.2ml/litre.

6. CUTWORMS (Wadudu wa kukata — Agrotis spp.)
   Crops affected: Maize, beans, tomatoes, vegetables (seedling stage)
   Signs: Young seedlings cut off at soil level overnight. Grubs (fat, curled, grey) 
   found in soil near cut plants.
   
   CULTURAL: Deep ploughing before planting exposes pupae to birds.
   CHEMICAL: Drench soil with Chlorpyrifos 480EC around seedling base.
             Or mix bran + molasses + insecticide bait and place near seedlings at dusk.

7. BEAN STEM MAGGOT (Ophiomyia spp.)
   Crops affected: Beans (especially common bean)
   Signs: Yellowing and dying of seedlings 10–14 days after emergence. 
   Black stripe on stem near soil. White maggots inside stem when cut open.
   
   TREATMENT: Seed dressing with Thiamethoxam before planting.
              Apply Imidacloprid 200SL as soil drench at emergence.

RODENT CONTROL
==============

Moles and Field Rats:
- Signs: Tunnels in soil, gnawed stems and roots, stores of grain found underground.
- Control:
  1. Traps: Victor-type snap traps baited with peanut butter or maize grain.
  2. Zinc Phosphide bait: Place in burrow entrances at dusk (WEAR GLOVES).
  3. Encourage owls and raptors — do not kill hawks near the farm.
  4. Peppermint oil on cotton balls placed in burrow openings.
  5. Plant lemongrass around field boundaries as repellent.

---

PESTICIDE SAFETY REMINDERS
============================
- Always wear PPE: gloves, mask, goggles, long-sleeved clothing.
- Do NOT spray when bees are active (morning). Spray at dusk.
- Observe Pre-Harvest Interval (PHI) — the waiting period between spraying and harvest.
  - Example: Cypermethrin PHI on tomatoes = 7 days.
  - Example: Dimethoate PHI on beans = 14 days.
- Store chemicals in lockable store away from children, food, water sources.
- Dispose of empty containers: puncture, rinse 3x, and bury in approved site.
- Never spray near rivers, ponds, or bee hives.

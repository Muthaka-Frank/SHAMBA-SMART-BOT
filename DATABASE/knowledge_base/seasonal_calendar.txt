KENYAN FARMING SEASONAL CALENDAR
===================================
Recommended Planting Dates by Region and Crop
Source: Kenya Meteorological Department + KALRO Recommendations

---

KENYA'S TWO MAIN RAINY SEASONS
================================

LONG RAINS (Masika / Mbura ya Njahi):
- Period: March – June (peak April–May)
- Reliable in: Central Highlands, Nyanza, Western Kenya, Rift Valley
- Best for: Maize, beans, potatoes, vegetables, sorghum

SHORT RAINS (Vuli / Mbura ya Mwere):
- Period: October – December (peak November)
- More variable and shorter than long rains
- Best for: Fast-maturing crops: beans, vegetables, tomatoes, sorghum

---

PLANTING CALENDAR BY REGION
=============================

CENTRAL HIGHLANDS (Kikuyu, Nyeri, Muranga, Kirinyaga, Embu)
Altitude: 1500–2500m | Good rainfall: 1000–1500mm/year

  Long Rains:
  - Maize (90-day variety): Plant mid-February to end March → Harvest June–July
  - Irish Potatoes: Plant February–March on ridges → Harvest May–June
  - Beans: Plant March–April → Harvest June
  - Cabbages, Spinach: Plant March → Harvest May–June
  - Peas (garden): Plant March–April → Harvest May–June

  Short Rains:
  - Beans: Plant October → Harvest December
  - Potatoes (second crop): Plant September–October → Harvest December
  - Tomatoes: Plant in September (transplant); Harvest January
  - Garlic and onions: Plant October → Harvest February

  Year-round (with irrigation):
  - Tomatoes, Kale, Spinach, Capsicum

RIFT VALLEY (Nakuru, Naivasha, Kericho, Nandi, Trans-Nzoia)
Altitude: 1000–2500m | Good rainfall

  Long Rains:
  - Maize (H614, DK8031): Plant February–March → Harvest July–August
  - Wheat: Plant March–April → Harvest August–September
  - Sunflower: Plant April → Harvest August
  - Beans: Plant March → Harvest June

  Short Rains:
  - Maize (short-season 60-day): Plant October → Harvest December
  - Vegetables, Tomatoes: October–November

EASTERN PROVINCE (Machakos, Kitui, Makueni — semi-arid)
Rainfall: 400–800mm/year | ASAL (Arid and Semi-Arid Land)

  Long Rains (often unreliable):
  - Sorghum (drought-tolerant): Plant April → Harvest August
  - Green grams (ndengu): Plant April → Harvest July
  - Cowpeas: Plant April → Harvest July
  - Pigeon peas (mbaazi): Plant March → Harvest August
  - Cassava: Plant April (tolerates drought)

  Short Rains:
  - Green grams: October → Harvest December
  - Cowpeas: October → Harvest December
  - Sweet potatoes: October → December

  IMPORTANT: Use drought-tolerant varieties only. 
  Plant on contour ridges to conserve moisture. Use mulching heavily.

NYANZA AND WESTERN KENYA (Kisumu, Homa Bay, Vihiga, Bungoma)
Altitude: 1000–1800m | Bimodal rainfall

  Long Rains:
  - Maize + Beans (intercrop): Plant March → Harvest July–August
  - Sorghum: Plant March → Harvest August
  - Groundnuts: Plant April → Harvest July
  - Tomatoes: Transplant March → Harvest May–June

  Short Rains:
  - Beans: October → Harvest December
  - Sweet potatoes: October → Harvest January
  - Cassava: October (long-season, harvest in 8–12 months)

COASTAL REGION (Mombasa, Kilifi, Kwale, Lamu)
Hot and humid | Two rainy seasons

  Long Rains (Masika): March–May
  - Maize: April → Harvest July
  - Cassava: April (harvest after 9–12 months)
  - Cowpeas: April → Harvest June

  Short Rains (Vuli): October–December
  - Tomatoes: October → Harvest January
  - Green grams: October → Harvest December
  - Vegetables: October → Harvest December

SOIL PREPARATION TIMING
========================
- Plough 4–6 weeks BEFORE planting — allow weeds to germinate and die.
- Second tillage: 2 weeks before planting to remove weed regrowth.
- Apply compost/manure when ploughing for best incorporation.
- Apply lime (if needed) at first ploughing — it needs 6–8 weeks to react.
- Make planting furrows/holes the day before planting.

---

PEST PRESSURE BY SEASON
========================
- Long rains: Higher Fall Armyworm pressure. Scout weekly starting 2 weeks after germination.
- Short rains: Higher Aphid and Whitefly pressure (drier conditions).
- Post-harvest (storage): Weevils in stored maize — treat with Actellic Super dust.
